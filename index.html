Key Insights
What this quarter's data is telling CTIE — and where to focus support next quarter.
📈
TRL Movement Is Healthy
59% of startups progressed in TRL this quarter. 3 startups regressed — Biosyl, Emileage, and Chemioptics — all flagged for individual review next quarter.
💰
Funding Is the #1 Constraint
Funding/runway appears in 33 constraint responses — the single most cited constraint across all stages. Multiple startups specifically requested SISF grant tranche 2 release.
🤝
Customer Access Gap is Real
25 startups cite customer access as a top constraint yet 31 had zero customer conversations this quarter. CTIE industry connect programs would directly address this gap.
⚠️
35% Have No Roadmap
18 startups had no quarterly roadmap going into this quarter. Without a roadmap, milestone tracking is impossible. CTIE should make roadmap submission mandatory at quarter start.
🏭
External Dependencies Dominate
53% of startups cite external dependencies as the primary execution blocker — government approvals, vendor delays, regulatory bodies. These are outside CTIE's direct control but can be mitigated through network introductions.
📞
Outreach Works — 25% No Response
Of 60 called, 21 committed to submit same day and 44 eventually submitted. 15 had no response — these need a second contact attempt or email follow-up before next quarter close.
🚀
Agastya Machineries — Top Performer
₹15.9L revenue, 11 customer conversations, 2 active pilots — the standout this quarter. Agastya should be featured in next cohort communications as a benchmark.
🔒
One Startup Closed Operations
Celium Devices Private Limited has closed operations — confirmed during outreach. Should be formally exited from the program and records updated in the incubation portal.
